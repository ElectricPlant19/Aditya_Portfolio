Turning TLE Data into
GNSS Health Intelligence

From raw Space-Track API ingestion to interpretable constellation health scores — how the pipeline works, what it monitors, and why it matters.

01
The Problem

Raw orbital data is unreadable.

GNSS constellations are critical infrastructure, but Two-Line Element records are dense, numeric, and opaque. Orbital drift, slot deviation, and station-keeping residuals are buried in state vectors — invisible without propagation, processing, and visualisation. There was no fast way to assess constellation health at a glance.

02
The Solution

An automated intelligence pipeline.

A Streamlit dashboard that pulls TLE data from the Space-Track API, propagates positions using SGP4 orbital mechanics, and computes a custom weighted health index — combining orbital drift magnitude, station-keeping residuals, and inclination deviation into a single interpretable per-satellite score.

Japan's QZSS After H3 Failure: Resilience by Design
Published: Apr 2026 Focus: QZSS · NavIC comparison

H3 Flight F8's second-stage failure stranded QZS-5 in a sub-optimal orbit, delaying Japan's 7-satellite expansion by ~18 months. The report analyses per-satellite longitude slot adherence (e.g. QZS-1R sitting 12.57° west of its 148° designation), April 2026 health scores across the active QZSS fleet, and a direct architectural comparison with NavIC — which now operates at 43% of design capacity vs QZSS's 71% despite both suffering recent satellite losses.

NavIC in November 2025: Performance Under Strain
Published: Nov 2025 Focus: NavIC · IRNSS fleet

Inaugural issue using open-source TLE data and a custom health-scoring methodology to assess India's NavIC fleet — then 4 of 7 satellites operational. IRNSS-1B and IRNSS-1I score 91.3 and 93.4, while the newer NVS-01 scores just 60.5 due to significant inclination deviation. Includes GDOP analysis of the 4-satellite geometry over Delhi and E-W station-keeping pattern assessment, revealing signs of careful propellant management ahead of constellation replenishment.

About

The person behind the pipeline.

I'm an Aerospace Engineering student at IIEST Shibpur interested in the intersection of orbital mechanics, satellite navigation, software, and data-driven space-domain analysis. My current work focuses on transforming raw orbital data into useful intelligence — especially for GNSS constellations like NavIC, QZSS, and BeiDou-3.

I like building things that sit between engineering and software: dashboards, analysis pipelines, visual tools, and technical reports that make complex orbital systems easier to understand.
